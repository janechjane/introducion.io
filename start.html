Introduction
The Weeknd is a Canadian singer, songwriter, and record producer, widely recognized for his distinctive voice and unique musical style. His real name is Abel Makkonen Tesfaye, and he was born on February 16, 1990, in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. He is of Ethiopian descent, and he was raised by his mother and grandmother after his parents separated when he was a child. The Weeknd first gained attention in 2010 when he anonymously uploaded several songs to YouTube under the name “The Weeknd.” In 2011, he released three critically acclaimed mixtapes: House of Balloons, Thursday, and Echoes of Silence. These mixtapes showcased his dark and atmospheric R&B sound, combined with mysterious and emotional lyrics, and quickly earned him a dedicated following. In 2013, he released his debut studio album Kiss Land, followed by Beauty Behind the Madness in 2015, which included global hits like Can’t Feel My Face and The Hills. These songs established him as a mainstream pop and R&B star. In 2016, he released Starboy, featuring the title track with Daft Punk, which won the Grammy Award for Best Urban Contemporary Album. One of his most successful songs, Blinding Lights from his 2020 album After Hours, became a worldwide hit and broke multiple records, including spending the most weeks in the Billboard Hot 100 Top 10. The song’s 80s-inspired synth-pop sound further showcased his versatility as an artist. In 2021, he performed at the Super Bowl LV halftime show, bringing his unique artistic vision to a massive global audience. The Weeknd’s music often explores themes of love, heartbreak, loneliness, fame, and the darker sides of life. His style blends R&B, pop, hip-hop, and alternative influences, and he is known for his haunting falsetto vocals and innovative production. Throughout his career, The Weeknd has received numerous awards, including multiple Grammy Awards, Billboard Music Awards, American Music Awards, and Juno Awards. He is regarded as one of the most influential and innovative artists of his generation, constantly pushing the boundaries of modern music with his cinematic storytelling, creative visuals, and genre-blending sound.

The Idol

June 4, 2023 (HBO U.S. premiere)

The Idol is a 2023 HBO series about Jocelyn, a pop star trying to make a comeback, and her dangerous relationship with Tedros, a nightclub owner and cult leader. Created by The Weeknd and Sam Levinson, the show explores fame, power, and the dark side of the music industry. It received mixed reviews but was praised for its bold storytelling.
